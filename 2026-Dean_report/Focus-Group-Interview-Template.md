# IMPACT — Focus Group Interview Template
## Advanced Behavioral Version | Spring 2026

**Purpose:** Collect behavioral evidence of student growth across all 5 AACSB learning outcomes in a single structured conversation. Replaces self-rating scales with specific-incident questions that produce verifiable, quotable evidence.
**Conducted by:** Team Lead or PM (1-on-1, ~20 minutes per student)
**When:** Final 2 weeks of semester
**Record:** Type or handwrite responses directly in this document. Submit to Operations by **[Deadline]**.
**AACSB note:** Responses serve as primary qualitative evidence for LO1–LO5. Direct quotes are cited in the Closing-the-Loop Memo.

---

## Interviewer Instructions

**Before the interview:**
- Review the student's weekly reports and any deliverables you've seen from them — it will help you prompt for specifics.
- You are looking for *evidence*, not impressions. Push for the name of a deliverable, the date of an event, the name of a person.

**During the interview:**
- If a student gives a vague answer ("I got better at communication"), probe: *"Give me a specific example — what did you write, who did you send it to, and what happened?"*
- Write down what the student actually said, not your interpretation of it. Quotes are more valuable than summaries.
- Do not lead. Do not tell them which answer you're looking for.

**After the interview:**
- Mark the LO rating for each section using the scale below.
- Submit the completed document to Operations.

---

## LO Rating Scale

Use this scale to rate each learning outcome after the interview, based on the evidence the student provided. This is your judgment as the observer — not the student's self-rating.

| Score | Label | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| 4 — Exceeds | Strong, specific evidence the student consistently demonstrated this competency | Multiple concrete examples; student can articulate growth |
| 3 — Meets | Adequate evidence the student demonstrated this competency | At least one clear example; student can describe what they did |
| 2 — Approaching | Limited or weak evidence; student demonstrated the competency inconsistently | Vague examples; student describes intent more than action |
| 1 — Does Not Yet Meet | No credible evidence; student could not provide a specific example | Student described others' work or spoke only in generalities |

> **Scoring rule:** If the student gives one strong specific example, the minimum score is 2. If they give two or more, it can be 3–4. Reserve 4 for students who demonstrate both breadth and articulation.

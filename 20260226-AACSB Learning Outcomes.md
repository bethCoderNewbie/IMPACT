# IMPACT — AACSB Assurance of Learning Framework

**Date:** 2026-02-26
**Program:** IMPACT — W. Frank Barton School of Business, Wichita State University
**Accreditation Standard:** AACSB International — Assurance of Learning (AOL)
**Linked Documents:** `20260226-Objectives and Metrics.md`, `20260226-Architecture Plan.md`

---

## What AACSB Requires

AACSB does not prescribe specific learning outcomes — it requires that the school:

1. **Define** learning goals appropriate to the program's mission
2. **Embed** assessments within the learning experience (not just surveys)
3. **Measure** student achievement against those goals
4. **Close the loop** — use results to improve the program, and document that improvement
5. **Maintain** a continuous evidence trail for the 5-year review cycle

IMPACT, as an applied learning initiative within a dual-accredited school, must demonstrate that participation produces **documented, measurable professional competencies** — not just experiential exposure.

---

## IMPACT Program Mission Alignment

**Barton School Mission:** Applied and experiential learning where students apply knowledge gained from faculty and business leaders while developing professional and life skills.

**IMPACT's contribution to that mission:** Students engage in real consulting engagements with Kansas businesses, applying cross-disciplinary business knowledge under PM supervision, and producing professional deliverables for actual clients.

**AOL scope:** IMPACT is an applied learning program, not a standalone course. The learning outcomes below are **program-level** outcomes, assessed through embedded instruments within the IMPACT workflow — not separate exams or coursework.

## The 5 Learning Outcomes

Each outcome is tied to AACSB's standard competency domains and mapped to a specific assessment instrument embedded in the IMPACT workflow.

---

### LO1 — Professional Communication

**Statement:** Students demonstrate effective written and oral communication tailored to a professional business audience.

**AACSB Domain:** Written and Oral Communication

**Behavioral Indicators (what "achieving" this looks like):**
- Deliverables are clearly written, free of jargon, and appropriate for the client audience
- Weekly task reports are complete, specific, and submitted on time
- Student can present project progress to leadership or the client without excessive prompting

**Assessment Instruments:**
| Instrument | When | Who Assesses | Recorded In |
|---|---|---|---|
| Self-assessment (pre) — Communication rating | Onboarding Form (Day 1) | Student | Student Roster — Pre-Assessment Score |
| Weekly task report quality | Every week | PM review flag | Weekly Task Reports — Reviewed by PM |
| PM evaluation rubric — Communication dimension | End of semester | Project Manager | Student Roster — PM Evaluation Score |
| Self-assessment (post) — Communication rating | Exit Form (last week) | Student | Student Roster — Post-Assessment Score |

**Achievement threshold:** PM Evaluation Score ≥ 3.5/5.0 on Communication dimension

---

### LO2 — Analytical & Critical Thinking

**Statement:** Students apply analytical frameworks and evidence-based reasoning to diagnose client problems and develop actionable recommendations.

**AACSB Domain:** Analytical Thinking / Critical Thinking

**Behavioral Indicators:**
- Student identifies root causes rather than symptoms in client challenges
- Recommendations in deliverables are supported by data, research, or structured analysis
- Student can defend their reasoning when questioned by PM or client

**Assessment Instruments:**
| Instrument | When | Who Assesses | Recorded In |
|---|---|---|---|
| Self-assessment (pre) — Analytical rating | Onboarding Form (Day 1) | Student | Student Roster — Pre-Assessment Score |
| Deliverable quality review | Per deliverable | PM | Deliverable Tracker (Planner rating) |
| PM evaluation rubric — Analytical dimension | End of semester | Project Manager | Student Roster — PM Evaluation Score |
| Self-assessment (post) — Analytical rating | Exit Form (last week) | Student | Student Roster — Post-Assessment Score |

**Achievement threshold:** PM Evaluation Score ≥ 3.5/5.0 on Analytical dimension

---

### LO3 — Project Management & Professionalism

**Statement:** Students demonstrate the ability to manage their time, meet deadlines, collaborate within a team, and conduct themselves with professional standards.

**AACSB Domain:** Teamwork / Leadership / Ethical Conduct

**Behavioral Indicators:**
- Weekly task reports submitted on time ≥ 90% of weeks
- Deliverables completed by agreed deadlines
- Positive peer and PM feedback on reliability and team contribution
- Adherence to client confidentiality and IMPACT SOPs

**Assessment Instruments:**
| Instrument | When | Who Assesses | Recorded In |
|---|---|---|---|
| Self-assessment (pre) — Professionalism rating | Onboarding Form (Day 1) | Student | Student Roster — Pre-Assessment Score |
| Report submission rate | Every week (automated) | Power Automate | Weekly Task Reports — Submission Timestamp |
| Deliverable on-time rate | Per deliverable | PM | Master Project List — Actual vs. Planned End Date |
| PM evaluation rubric — Professionalism dimension | End of semester | Project Manager | Student Roster — PM Evaluation Score |
| Self-assessment (post) — Professionalism rating | Exit Form (last week) | Student | Student Roster — Post-Assessment Score |

**Achievement threshold:** PM Evaluation Score ≥ 3.5/5.0 on Professionalism dimension AND report submission rate ≥ 85%

---

### LO4 — Applied Business Knowledge

**Statement:** Students apply concepts from their business education (e.g., marketing, finance, operations, strategy) to solve real problems for Kansas businesses.

**AACSB Domain:** Application of Business Knowledge / Discipline-Specific Knowledge

**Behavioral Indicators:**
- Deliverables reference appropriate business frameworks or disciplinary knowledge
- Student can articulate how their major coursework connects to their IMPACT project work
- Client feedback indicates that recommendations were practically useful and grounded

**Assessment Instruments:**
| Instrument | When | Who Assesses | Recorded In |
|---|---|---|---|
| Self-assessment (pre) — Applied knowledge rating | Onboarding Form (Day 1) | Student | Student Roster — Pre-Assessment Score |
| Client satisfaction score | End of project | Client (via feedback form) | Master Project List — Client Satisfaction Score |
| PM evaluation rubric — Applied Knowledge dimension | End of semester | Project Manager | Student Roster — PM Evaluation Score |
| Self-assessment (post) — Applied knowledge rating | Exit Form (last week) | Student | Student Roster — Post-Assessment Score |

**Achievement threshold:** PM Evaluation Score ≥ 3.5/5.0 on Applied Knowledge dimension AND Client Satisfaction Score ≥ 4.0/5.0

---

### LO5 — Ethical & Stakeholder Awareness

**Statement:** Students recognize ethical dimensions in business decisions and demonstrate awareness of obligations to multiple stakeholders — clients, community, peers, and the institution.

**AACSB Domain:** Ethical Understanding and Reasoning

**Behavioral Indicators:**
- Student respects client confidentiality throughout the engagement
- Student raises concerns about ethically ambiguous situations rather than ignoring them
- Deliverables consider impact on stakeholders beyond the immediate client contact
- No violations of IMPACT SOPs related to data handling, communication, or conduct

**Assessment Instruments:**
| Instrument | When | Who Assesses | Recorded In |
|---|---|---|---|
| Self-assessment (pre) — Ethics/Stakeholder rating | Onboarding Form (Day 1) | Student | Student Roster — Pre-Assessment Score |
| SOP acknowledgment completion | Week 1 | Operations | Onboarding Tracker — Training Completed |
| PM evaluation rubric — Ethics dimension | End of semester | Project Manager | Student Roster — PM Evaluation Score |
| Self-assessment (post) — Ethics/Stakeholder rating | Exit Form (last week) | Student | Student Roster — Post-Assessment Score |

**Achievement threshold:** PM Evaluation Score ≥ 3.5/5.0 on Ethics dimension AND Training Completed = Yes

## Assessment Instruments (Full Specification)

### Instrument 1: Student Self-Assessment (Pre & Post)
**Delivered via:** Microsoft Forms
**Administered:** Day 1 (Pre) and final week (Post)
**Scored:** 1–5 Likert scale per competency

| Question | Competency | Scale |
|---|---|---|
| How confident are you in your ability to communicate professionally in writing to a business audience? | LO1 | 1 (Not at all) → 5 (Very confident) |
| How confident are you in your ability to analyze a business problem and develop evidence-based recommendations? | LO2 | 1 → 5 |
| How confident are you in your ability to manage your time and meet professional deadlines? | LO3 | 1 → 5 |
| How well can you apply concepts from your business courses to real-world problems? | LO4 | 1 → 5 |
| How aware are you of your ethical obligations to clients, peers, and your institution? | LO5 | 1 → 5 |

**Aggregate Pre-Assessment Score** = average of 5 ratings → stored in `Student Roster — Pre-Assessment Score`
**Aggregate Post-Assessment Score** = average of 5 ratings → stored in `Student Roster — Post-Assessment Score`
**Skill Delta** = Post − Pre → positive delta is the evidence of learning

---

### Instrument 2: PM Evaluation Rubric
**Delivered via:** Microsoft Forms (PM-only link)
**Administered:** Final week of semester
**Scored:** 1–5 per dimension

| Dimension | 1 — Does Not Meet | 3 — Meets | 5 — Exceeds |
|---|---|---|---|
| **LO1 Communication** | Deliverables unclear; reports incomplete or late frequently | Clear deliverables; reports submitted on time most weeks | Exceptional clarity; proactively communicates blockers and updates |
| **LO2 Analytical Thinking** | Recommendations unsupported; surface-level analysis | Sound reasoning with some evidence; practical recommendations | Rigorous analysis; insights go beyond what was asked |
| **LO3 Professionalism** | Missed deadlines; unreliable; required frequent reminders | Generally reliable; met most commitments | Consistently exceeded commitments; positive team presence |
| **LO4 Applied Knowledge** | Little connection between coursework and project work | Applies relevant frameworks appropriately | Brings in frameworks unprompted; client noted knowledge quality |
| **LO5 Ethics & Stakeholders** | Disregarded confidentiality or stakeholder concerns | Respected norms; completed SOP training | Proactively raised ethical considerations; role model behavior |

**PM Evaluation Score** = average of 5 dimensions → stored in `Student Roster — PM Evaluation Score`

---

### Instrument 3: Client Feedback Form
**Delivered via:** Microsoft Forms (sent by Operations post-project)
**Administered:** Within 2 weeks of project completion

| Question | Maps To | Scale |
|---|---|---|
| How useful were the recommendations or deliverables your team received? | LO4 | 1–5 |
| How professionally did the team communicate throughout the engagement? | LO1 | 1–5 |
| How likely are you to engage IMPACT again for a future project? | Overall satisfaction | 1–5 (NPS proxy) |
| What was the most valuable aspect of the engagement? | Qualitative | Open text |
| What could have been done better? | Qualitative | Open text |

## Scoring & Achievement Summary

For AACSB reporting, achievement is determined at the **cohort level**, not individual level.

| Learning Outcome | Achievement Threshold | Target Achievement Rate |
|---|---|---|
| LO1 — Professional Communication | PM score ≥ 3.5 on Communication dimension | ≥ 80% of students |
| LO2 — Analytical Thinking | PM score ≥ 3.5 on Analytical dimension | ≥ 80% of students |
| LO3 — Professionalism | PM score ≥ 3.5 AND submission rate ≥ 85% | ≥ 85% of students |
| LO4 — Applied Knowledge | PM score ≥ 3.5 AND Client Satisfaction ≥ 4.0 | ≥ 80% of students |
| LO5 — Ethics & Awareness | PM score ≥ 3.5 AND Training Completed = Yes | ≥ 90% of students |

## Closed-Loop Process (AACSB Requirement)

AACSB requires evidence that assessment results are used to improve the program. This is the "closing the loop" step — the most commonly cited gap in accreditation reviews.

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  1. COLLECT                                             │
│     Pre/Post self-assessments + PM rubrics + Client     │
│     feedback → aggregated in SharePoint Lists           │
└────────────────────────┬────────────────────────────────┘
                         ▼
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  2. ANALYZE (end of each semester)                      │
│     Operations + Faculty Liaison review:                │
│     - Which LOs fell below target achievement rate?     │
│     - Which teams or cohorts underperformed?            │
│     - Is the skill delta positive across the cohort?    │
└────────────────────────┬────────────────────────────────┘
                         ▼
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  3. DECIDE (leadership + faculty)                       │
│     - Update SOP or training for underperforming LO?    │
│     - Adjust PM rubric if scoring is inconsistent?      │
│     - Add a mid-semester check-in if delta is small?    │
└────────────────────────┬────────────────────────────────┘
                         ▼
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  4. IMPLEMENT (next semester)                           │
│     Changes made to SOPs, training, or assessment tools │
│     are documented in the SOP Library with change log   │
└────────────────────────┬────────────────────────────────┘
                         ▼
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  5. DOCUMENT (continuous)                               │
│     Semester AOL Summary stored in Hub SOP Library:     │
│     - Aggregate scores by LO                            │
│     - Achievement rates vs. targets                     │
│     - Improvement actions taken                         │
│     - Evidence of change in next semester results       │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## AOL Documentation Requirements (Per Semester)

Each semester, the following must be filed in the Hub SOP Library under `AOL Records > [Semester Year]`:

| Document | Contents | Owner | Deadline |
|---|---|---|---|
| AOL Data Export | All PM evaluation scores + self-assessment deltas from SharePoint | Operations | 2 weeks after semester ends |
| AOL Achievement Summary | Cohort achievement rates per LO vs. targets | Faculty Liaison | 3 weeks after semester ends |
| Closed-Loop Action Record | What underperformed → what decision was made → what changed | Faculty Liaison + Leadership | Before next semester begins |
| SOP Change Log | Which SOPs were updated as a result of AOL findings | Operations | Before next semester begins |

## Roles & Responsibilities

| Role | AOL Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Operations Team | Administer forms, collect data, export results, file documents |
| Project Manager (per team) | Complete PM evaluation rubric for each student by deadline |
| Faculty Liaison (Barton faculty) | Review aggregate results, make improvement decisions, sign off on AOL Summary |
| Leadership | Approve changes to program structure based on AOL findings |
| AACSB Coordinator (Barton School) | Incorporate IMPACT AOL data into school-wide accreditation reports |

## Open Questions for Leadership & Faculty

| # | Question | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| A1 | Who is the designated Faculty Liaison for IMPACT's AOL process? | Required for AACSB — a faculty member must own the learning outcome review |
| A2 | Are the 5 LOs above aligned with Barton's school-level learning goals? | IMPACT's LOs should map to the school's existing AOL framework, not contradict it |
| A3 | Should the PM rubric be peer-reviewed by faculty before first use? | Ensures scoring consistency and AACSB defensibility |
| A4 | What is the minimum cohort size for AOL reporting? | If a semester has fewer than 10 students, aggregate reporting may need adjustment |
| A5 | Should LO achievement be reported per team or across all teams combined? | Per-team reporting reveals more but requires larger sample size per team |
| A6 | Is there an existing Barton AOL committee this data should feed into? | Avoids duplicating effort; IMPACT data may slot into existing reporting cycle |

## Objective 1: Student Learning & Development
*Reporting to: AACSB, WSU Administration, Legislature*

### Objective Statement
Every student who participates in IMPACT develops and demonstrates professional and applied learning competencies that can be documented and reported for accreditation and program evaluation.

### Key Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Target | Data Source | Cadence |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Students served | Total unique students engaged per semester | ≥ 25 (current) → ≥ 45 (at scale) | Student Roster (SharePoint List) | Semester |
| Majors represented | Count of distinct academic majors in the program | ≥ 5 disciplines | Student Roster — Major field | Semester |
| Applied learning hours | Total hours logged by all students on IMPACT projects | TBD by leadership | Weekly task reports | Semester |
| Training completion rate | % of students who complete all required SOP training by Week 2 | ≥ 90% | Onboarding Tracker — Training checklist | Semester |
| Skill self-assessment delta | Pre/post score change on self-assessment (skills rubric) | Positive delta ≥ 1 point avg | Onboarding Form (pre) + Exit Form (post) | Per student |
| Student retention rate | % of students who complete the full semester engagement | ≥ 85% | Student Roster — Status field | Semester |
| Success story rate | % of alumni who secured an internship or job linked to IMPACT experience | TBD | Success Stories List | Annual |
| Graduation pipeline | Students graduating within 1 semester of IMPACT participation | Tracked | Student Roster — Grad date field | Annual |

### AACSB-Specific Requirements
AACSB Assurance of Learning (AOL) requires that learning outcomes be:
1. **Defined** — document the learning outcomes IMPACT is designed to produce (e.g., professional communication, project management, analytical reasoning)
2. **Measured** — self-assessments and PM evaluations serve as measurement instruments
3. **Closed-loop** — program must show how results feed back into program improvements

**Action:** Define 3–5 specific learning outcomes for IMPACT in collaboration with Barton faculty. Map each outcome to a measurable assessment in the onboarding/exit forms.

## Objective 2: Business Partnership & Community Impact
*Reporting to: Kansas State Legislature, WSU Administration*

### Objective Statement
IMPACT advances business partnerships that create measurable economic and operational value for Kansas businesses, justifying the state appropriation under fund 1000-0030.

### Key Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Target | Data Source | Cadence |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Active business partners | Count of unique client organizations engaged per semester | ≥ 5 (current) → ≥ 9 (at scale) | Master Project List | Semester |
| New partners acquired | New client organizations that have not previously engaged IMPACT | ≥ 2/semester | Master Project List — First engagement date | Semester |
| Partner retention rate | % of prior clients who re-engage for another project | ≥ 50% | Master Project List — Repeat flag | Annual |
| Projects delivered | Total completed projects per semester | ≥ 5 → ≥ 9 | Master Project List — Status = Completed | Semester |
| Client satisfaction score | Average rating on post-project client feedback form | ≥ 4.0 / 5.0 | Microsoft Forms — Client feedback | Per project |
| Estimated value delivered | Applied consulting hours × market rate proxy ($50–$75/hr) | Report annually | Weekly task reports × hours field | Annual |
| Kansas-based partners | % of clients headquartered or operating in Kansas | ≥ 80% | Master Project List — Location field | Annual |
| Industry sectors represented | Count of distinct industries served | ≥ 3 sectors | Master Project List — Industry field | Semester |

### Legislative Reporting Note
The $5M appropriation is tied to *"advancing business partnerships and applied learning initiatives."* At minimum, the annual report to the Legislature must demonstrate:
- A quantified number of business partnerships created or sustained
- A quantified number of students who received applied learning experience
- Evidence of economic benefit to Kansas (value delivered, jobs-adjacent outcomes)

**Estimated value delivered is the single most compelling metric for the Legislature.** If 9 teams × 5 students × 15 hrs/week × 16 weeks = 10,800 student hours at a market proxy of $50/hr = **$540,000 in consulting value delivered** per semester. That is the story to tell.

## Objective 3: Operational Excellence
*Reporting to: Internal Leadership, WSU Administration*

### Objective Statement
IMPACT operations run efficiently, with consistent processes that scale from 5 to 9 teams without proportional increases in operational overhead.

### Key Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Target | Data Source | Cadence |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Onboarding time | Days from HR clearance email to student's first project contribution | ≤ 5 business days | Onboarding Tracker — Date fields | Per cohort |
| Weekly report submission rate | % of students who submit weekly task reports on time | ≥ 90% | Deliverables List — submission timestamp | Weekly |
| Deliverable on-time rate | % of project deliverables completed by the agreed deadline | ≥ 85% | Master Project List — Deliverable due vs. completed dates | Semester |
| SOP coverage | % of active SOPs that have been reviewed and updated in the last 6 months | 100% | SOP Library — Last modified date | Semester |
| Meeting minutes filed | % of team meetings with minutes filed within 48 hours | ≥ 90% | Meeting Minutes library — upload timestamp | Semester |
| Operational overhead ratio | Hours spent on admin/coordination vs. hours on client work | Reduce each semester | Weekly task reports — category field | Semester |
| New team provisioning time | Hours from IT ticket submission to team fully operational | ≤ 48 hours total | IT ticket log + Provisioning checklist | Per team |

## Objective 4: Financial Stewardship
*Reporting to: Kansas State Legislature, WSU Administration*

### Objective Statement
IMPACT demonstrates responsible use of the $5M state appropriation by tracking cost per outcome and increasing efficiency as the program scales.

### Key Metrics

| Metric | Definition | How to Calculate | Cadence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cost per student served | Total program cost ÷ students served | Requires budget data from Barton leadership | Annual |
| Cost per business partner | Total program cost ÷ active business partners | Requires budget data | Annual |
| Cost per project delivered | Total program cost ÷ projects completed | Requires budget data | Annual |
| Value-to-cost ratio | Estimated value delivered ÷ program operating cost | Est. value / operating budget | Annual |
| Appropriation utilization rate | % of $5M actually drawn and spent on eligible activities | Finance + program records | Annual |

> **Note:** Full financial metrics require access to the program's operating budget, which is held by Barton leadership or WSU Finance. Operations team should request a budget summary figure each semester to complete these calculations.

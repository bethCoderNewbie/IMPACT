**TO:** IMPACT Leadership & Operations Teams
**FROM:** 
**DATE:** 2026-03-02
**SUBJECT:** Discussion Memo: Finalizing the "Impact Data Home" & Operational Roadmap

### 1. Meeting Objective
**Goal:** To align on the transition from manual, disparate systems to a centralized Microsoft 365 environment and define the priorities for onboarding automation.

**Non-Goal:** We are not discussing long-term budget allocations or technical backend configuration beyond the immediate M365 implementation.

### 2. Executive Summary & Context
*   **The Situation:** IMPACT currently relies on disparate systems like Trello and personal emails to track project work and student data. We have established a unified email address (`barton.impact@wichita.edu`) as a first step toward centralization.
*   **The Complication:** Manual onboarding and fragmented project tracking make it difficult to capture student metrics (majors, graduation dates) and "success stories" consistently. We are limited by our current Microsoft Office 365 licenses and need a solution that fits this constraint.
*   **The Core Question:** How do we structure the "Impact Data Home" on SharePoint to effectively automate communications, tracking, and onboarding?
    **Category A: Infrastructure & Asset Management**
    **Category B: Automated Workflows & Digitized Onboarding**
    **Category C: Project Tracking & Student Impact Metrics**

### 3. MECE Breakdown of the Topic

**Category A: Infrastructure & Asset Management**
*   **Context:** The team plans to build a centralized SharePoint "home base" for SOPs, media/marketing assets (headshots, branding).
*   **Current State:** Files, SOPs, and project data are currently scattered across personal drives, individual OneDrives, Microsoft Teams, and Trello boards. Administrative files (rosters, evaluations) are in an "Operations" folder split between Teams and OneDrive. Media assets are being consolidated into a shared Teams folder for website development access.
*   **Questions to Verify Current State:** 
    *   Can we confirm that all historical project data not currently "active" can be moved to a single "Archive" folder without further sorting?
    *   Who are the primary owners of the current "Operations" folders on Teams and OneDrive who can verify the final "source of truth" for rosters and evaluations?
    *   Are there any media assets (headshots, logos) currently stored on personal devices that have not yet been uploaded to the shared Teams folder?
*   **Key Discussion Point:** Establish a "Least Complex" M365 architecture that scales from 5 to 9+ teams without manual overhead.
*   **Pros/Cons:**
    *   **Pros:** Eliminates technical debt; reduces administrative burden as the team grows; utilizes existing licenses.
    *   **Cons:** Requires a one-time migration effort; necessitates strict adherence to naming and folder conventions.

**Category B: Automated Workflows & Digitized Onboarding**
*   **Context:** The goal is to route emails from `barton.impact@wichita.edu` automatically into SharePoint and move onboarding (HR paperwork, self-assessments) to a digital platform.
*   **Questions to Verify Current State:**
    *   What are the most frequent "manual triage" actions currently taken on the `barton.impact@wichita.edu` inbox that can be categorized (e.g., student inquiry, client lead)?
    *   What specific data fields are currently collected manually from new students that are not yet part of a digital form?
*   **Key Discussion Point:** How should we define the auto-routing rules for client requests versus student applications to ensure timely responses and scheduling?
*   **Pros/Cons:**
    *   **Pros:** Replaces manual email triage; professionalizes the first contact with students and clients.
    *   **Cons:** Technical setup of routing rules required; potential for errors if inbound subject lines are non-standard.

**Category C: Project Tracking & Student Impact Metrics**
*   **Context:** We are moving from Trello to templated weekly task reports and a centralized system to track meeting minutes and student milestones (graduation, training, internships).
*   **Questions to Verify Current State:**
    *   How many active projects are currently tracked in Trello that would need to be manually re-established in the new "Weekly Report" format?
    *   What is the current format of "Success Stories" (e.g., ad-hoc emails, verbal reports) and who is the designated collector for this data?
*   **Key Discussion Point:** What specific deliverables must be captured in the weekly reports to accurately demonstrate the program's impact?
*   **Pros/Cons:**
    *   **Pros:** Standardized visibility across all projects; easy extraction of "Success Stories" for reporting.
    *   **Cons:** Increased weekly administrative requirements for students; requires consistent PM review of minutes.

### 4. Required Input from Attendees
*   **Operations Team:** Please bring a list of all currently used password-protected accounts for the Excel migration, as well as an inventory of existing SOPs and media assets stored in personal folders.
*   **Leadership Team:** Please be prepared to discuss the timeline for decommissioning Trello and personal email for project work.
*   **Team Leads:** Please review the "Success Stories" capture requirements and be ready to discuss what qualifies as a reportable outcome.

### 5. Proposed Next Steps (Post-Discussion)
*   **Action 1:** Finalize the SharePoint site structure for the "Impact Data Home."
*   **Action 2:** Design the digital onboarding self-assessment form.
*   **Action 3:** Create the template for weekly task reports.

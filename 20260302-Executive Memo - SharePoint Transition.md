**SUBJECT:** Recommendation to centralize IMPACT operations on a SharePoint "Hub" to achieve 2x scale and $1M+ annual value reporting

### Executive Summary
* **Situation:** IMPACT currently supports ~25-30 students across 5 teams using fragmented tools (Trello, personal emails) and manual data entry for project tracking and onboarding.
* **Complication:** The program targets a 100% scale-up to 9+ teams (~54 students). Current manual overhead and data silos risk reporting inaccuracies for the $5M annual state appropriation and jeopardize AACSB accreditation data integrity.
* **Resolution:** We should implement the 3-tier M365 SharePoint "Impact Data Home" architecture to centralize all project, student, and legislative data while automating intake via the unified `barton.impact@wichita.edu` email.

### Supporting Analysis

**1. Scalability through Operational Excellence**
* **Template-First Design:** Transitioning from disparate Trello boards to a templated M365 Teams/Planner architecture reduces new team provisioning time to ~45 minutes, down from multiple hours of manual setup.
* **Centralized SOPs:** A Tier 1 SharePoint Hub ensures 100% of teams operate from a single source of truth for standard operating procedures (SOPs), headshots, and branding assets, eliminating "version drift" in project deliverables.

**2. Quantifiable Legislative & Economic Impact**
* **Value Delivered Metric:** Centralized weekly task reports will track "Applied Learning Hours." At 9 teams, IMPACT is projected to deliver ~10,800 hours per semester, translating to an estimated **$540,000 in market-value consulting** ($1.08M+ annually) for Kansas businesses.
* **Legislative Justification:** This data provides the primary evidence required by the Kansas State Legislature to justify the $5M appropriation (Fund 1000-0030) by quantifying business partnerships and student career readiness.

**3. Accreditation & Compliance (AACSB/FERPA)**
* **AACSB Assurance of Learning:** Standardized pre/post-assessment lists in SharePoint enable continuous "closed-loop" reporting of student competency gains, a critical requirement for dual-accreditation reviews.
* **FERPA-Compliant Success Stories:** The Tier 1 "Success Stories" list will utilize item-level permissions to ensure only student-consented stories are visible for marketing and donor engagement, protecting student privacy while maximizing program visibility.

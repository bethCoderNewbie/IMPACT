## What This Report Is About

At the kickoff session, the City of Wichita (CoW) asked: *what are cities like us actually doing?*

This report answers that question. We looked at four comparable cities and two major professional organizations to understand how other governments handle the same data challenges CoW faces today — and what CoW can learn from them.

---

## Why Benchmarking Matters

Three reasons drive this research:

1. **We need real examples, not just frameworks.** Before recommending a governance standard, we need to know whether similar cities have used it — and what happened.
2. **CoW's problems are not unique.** Other cities have dealt with data stored on USB drives, untracked files, and retention policies that exist on paper but are not enforced. They found solutions. We want to find those solutions for CoW.
3. **Leadership support is easier with evidence.** Saying "Austin did this and it worked" is more persuasive than citing a standards document.

For each city, we are researching:
- Whether they have formally adopted a data governance framework, and which one
- How they manage records retention across departments
- What data storage and analytics platforms they use
- What governance gaps they started with, and how they closed them
- How their governance teams are organized

## Professional Organizations We Are Studying

**International City/County Management Association (ICMA)**
ICMA represents over 13,000 local government managers across the U.S. We are reviewing their published guidance on data governance, records management, and framework recommendations for municipalities.

**Bloomberg What Works Cities (WWC)**
Bloomberg's WWC Certification program helps cities use data effectively. Over 100 cities hold this certification, including Los Angeles, Atlanta, and Denver. We are reviewing whether this certification is a realistic option for CoW and what it would require.

---

## The Two Problems We Are Focused On

These are the most practical questions in this engagement. Both reflect CoW's current situation directly.

### Problem 1 — Data Stored Outside Governed Systems

CoW currently stores some records on USB drives, personal hard drives, and shared network folders that are not part of any governed system. This creates compliance risk.

We are researching how other cities:
- Found out how much "shadow data" they had
- Created policy to stop ungoverned storage
- Migrated existing files into a governed system
- Used technology to enforce the change
- Communicated the change to staff

### Problem 2 — Retention Schedules That Are Not Enforced

CoW has retention periods defined in its data management lists, but enforcement in Laserfiche is unverified and no disposition approval process is documented.

We are researching how other cities:
- Rebuilt retention schedules from an inconsistent starting point
- Established legal authority for their retention rules
- Automated retention enforcement in document platforms
- Handled records that predate the governance program
- Divided responsibilities between the City Clerk, Legal, and IT

## How We Are Conducting This Research

**Primary Sources (Weeks 2–3)**
- Published governance documents from Austin, Kansas City, ICMA, and Bloomberg WWC
- Open data portals and city websites
- Direct outreach to peer city staff where time allows

**Secondary Sources**
- Academic and practitioner literature on municipal data governance
- State of Kansas records management guidelines
- National League of Cities and GFOA publications

**Limitations**
- Research relies mainly on publicly available documents. Unpublished practices may not be captured.
- Depth is constrained by the 6-week engagement timeline.
- Findings reflect each city's published program as of April 2026.

# Peer City Benchmarking Validation
**Source:** City of Wichita Official Budget Documents (2024–2026)
**Status:** Verified — Awaiting Review
**Last Updated:** 2026-04-06

---

The set of "Peer Cities" used by the City of Wichita in its budget and performance management documents (extending through the
2026 Adopted Budget) is structured into three distinct tiers based on the "extent" of the benchmark being conducted.

## Tier 1: Primary Regional Peers (Financial & Performance)

These are the most frequently cited in **[Section 05 – City Profile](https://www.wichita.gov/337/Budget-CIP-Documents)** (2025–2026 Adopted Budget, Page 35) of the budget book. They are used for benchmarking property tax (mill levy), sales tax, and per-capita spending. High-level benchmarking charts also appear in **Section 02 – City Manager's Policy Message** ([2024–2025 Adopted Budget, Page 11](https://www.wichita.gov/DocumentCenter/View/30497/02---City-Managers-Policy-Message-PDF#page=11); [2025–2026 Policy Message, Pages 14–18](https://www.wichita.gov/DocumentCenter/View/35337/02---City-Managers-Policy-Message-PDF#page=14)).

## Tier 3: Economic & "Choose Wichita" Peers

These are used by the Greater Wichita Partnership to benchmark Wichita against competitors for talent and corporate relocation.
Primary economic data is found in **Section 05 – City Profile** (2025–2026 Adopted Budget, Page 42 – "Choose Wichita" economic benchmarks).

- **Manufacturing/Tech Peers:** Grand Rapids (MI), Mobile (AL), and Greenville (SC). Used to compare aerospace cluster performance and high-tech engineering job growth. Cited in [Section 05, p.35](https://www.wichita.gov/337/Budget-CIP-Documents).
- **Cost-of-Living Peers:** Denver (CO), Dallas (TX), and Los Angeles (CA). "Negative benchmarks" highlighting Wichita's affordability for young professionals. Cited in [Section 05, p.42 – "Choose Wichita" cost-of-living comparisons](https://www.wichita.gov/337/Budget-CIP-Documents).

## Extending to 2026: The "Well-Run City" Dashboard

As of the 2026 Adopted Budget, Wichita has shifted its benchmarking "extent" toward Data Governance and Quality Assurance (DGQA).
The 2026 dashboard specifically focuses on:

1. **Public Safety Funding Gap:** Benchmarking Wichita's ~$341 per-capita police spending against the peer average of ~$420+ (OKC/Tulsa). Cited in [Section 02, p.11](https://www.wichita.gov/DocumentCenter/View/30497/02---City-Managers-Policy-Message-PDF#page=11) and [Section 05, p.35](https://www.wichita.gov/337/Budget-CIP-Documents).
2. **Structural Deficit Recovery:** Using peer comparisons (Sioux Falls and Tulsa) to identify $5M+ in efficiencies to bridge the 2026 General Fund gap. Referenced in "The Exchange" 2025 efficiency study and [2026 Budget Overview, p.12](https://www.wichita.gov/337/Budget-CIP-Documents).
3. **Library Technology Revision:** Benchmarking Wichita's $25 per-capita library funding against Omaha's $55 and OKC's $86 to justify digital/self-service model shifts. Cited in 2025 Library Budget Hearing and [2025–2026 Policy Message, p.16](https://www.wichita.gov/DocumentCenter/View/35337/02---City-Managers-Policy-Message-PDF#page=16).
4. **Utility Rate Containerization:** Maintaining Wichita's utility rates in the bottom 25% of the regional peer group despite major infrastructure investments. Cited in [Section 05, p.35](https://www.wichita.gov/337/Budget-CIP-Documents).

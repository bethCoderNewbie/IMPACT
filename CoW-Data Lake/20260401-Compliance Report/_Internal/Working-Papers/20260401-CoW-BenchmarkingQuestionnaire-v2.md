# CoW Data Governance Benchmarking Questionnaire — v2
**Prepared by:** IMPACT Consulting — Internal Use Only
**Date:** April 1, 2026
**Version:** v2 — Revised from v1 based on internal critique
**Purpose:** Structured questions for CoW stakeholders to shape the scope, depth, and decision criteria for the Peer City & Industry Benchmarking research feeding into Deliverable 1 (Standards Selection Brief)

> **How to use:** These questions are asked of CoW stakeholders in Week 1 kickoff sessions and early Week 2 interviews. They are not a survey — use them as a structured conversation guide. Capture answers in the interview notes; flag any unanswered items for follow-up before Week 2 research begins.
>
> **What this questionnaire does NOT cover:** Challenge 2 retention questions (legal source of truth, Laserfiche disposition triggers, and audit history) are already in the kickoff brief (City Clerk Q1, Q2, Q5). Capture those there. Do not re-ask them here.

---

## Section I — Peer City Research Priorities
**Goal:** Ensure the benchmarking targets the cities CoW leadership respects, surfaces informal intelligence, and identifies existing peer relationships we can use for direct outreach.
**Ask:** CoW project lead, City Clerk, or IT leadership

### Q1 — Primary Peer
Of the four confirmed peer cities — Kansas City, Tulsa, Omaha, Austin — which does CoW leadership most frequently use for operational comparisons (budget, policy, or infrastructure)?

> **Why this matters:** If leadership already has a strong view of one city, we allocate deeper research there and present those findings prominently in Deliverable 1.

---

### Q2 — Informal Intelligence on Peer Practices
Has CoW staff heard — through a conference, a peer network, or an informal conversation — about a specific department or program in any of these four cities that handles data governance or records management particularly well? Even a vague reference (e.g., "I heard Austin's finance team has a good system") is useful.

> **Why this matters:** Informal peer intelligence often points us to the most relevant and specific documentation faster than a cold search. We are not asking CoW to designate a "gold standard" — we are asking what they have already heard so we can investigate it directly.

---

### Q3 — Existing Relationships with Peer City Staff
Does anyone at CoW have an existing working relationship with IT staff, records managers, or data governance personnel at any of the four peer cities — through ICMA, a regional association, a conference, or a direct connection?

> **Why this matters:** Direct outreach to a peer city contact is the highest-value research method available to us. A 20-minute conversation with someone who has actually implemented a governance program yields more actionable detail than any published document. If a relationship exists, we can act on it immediately in Week 2.

---

### Q4 — Laserfiche Peer Precedent
Is CoW aware of any peer city — including cities outside the four confirmed peers — that has already gone through a data governance implementation specifically within Laserfiche Cloud?

> **Why this matters:** A city that has already configured Laserfiche for retention enforcement, security tagging, and disposition workflows is directly applicable to CoW's Deliverable 3 system requirements. This is the most specific and actionable peer precedent we could find.

---

## Section II — Professional Association Standards
**Goal:** Understand CoW's existing relationship with ICMA and Bloomberg WWC before we research what they recommend — so we are not presenting something CoW has already evaluated and rejected.
**Ask:** City Manager, City Clerk, or IT leadership

### Q1 — Bloomberg What Works Cities Status
Is the City of Wichita currently a member of, or actively pursuing, Bloomberg Philanthropies' What Works Cities certification? If so, what is the current status of that effort?

> **Why this matters:** If CoW is already on a path toward WWC certification, that resolves the standards selection: WWC becomes the primary framework and DAMA becomes the supporting implementation reference. This single answer could simplify Deliverable 1 significantly. If CoW has never heard of WWC, we spend more time explaining it in the brief.

---

### Q2 — ICMA Data Governance Guidance
Has CoW staff used any ICMA resources, tools, or publications specifically related to data governance, records management, or metadata standards when developing CoW's current practices? If so, which ones — and did the guidance inform any specific policy or procedure?

> **Why this matters:** If CoW has already applied ICMA guidance to build its current framework, we need to know which guidance and what it produced — so we can assess whether ICMA's recommendations have already been tested at CoW and where they fell short. If CoW has not engaged ICMA on governance specifically, that is a gap we can address in Deliverable 1.

---

## Section III — Shadow Data: Ungoverned Storage on USB Drives and Local Hard Drives
**Goal:** Establish factual ground truth about the scope of CoW's shadow data problem before comparing it to peer solutions.
**Ask:** IT Department / CISO

### Q1 — Known Inventory of Ungoverned Storage
Has IT ever conducted any form of inventory — formal or informal — to identify what data exists on USB drives, personal hard drives, or shared network drives outside Laserfiche? If so, what did it find? If not, is there any departmental awareness of which departments are most likely to have ungoverned local copies of official records?

> **Why this matters:** Peer city research will surface detection methodologies — but if CoW has already attempted an inventory, we need to know what method was used and whether it produced usable data. If no inventory has been attempted, that baseline gap is itself a finding in Deliverable 2.

---

### Q2 — IT Endpoint Controls
Does CoW currently use endpoint management software — such as Microsoft Intune, Group Policy, or a comparable tool — that has the technical capability to restrict or audit USB storage access if a policy were enacted? Is that capability currently active, inactive, or unknown?

> **Why this matters:** Peer city research will show a range of technical enforcement approaches. Whether CoW already has the infrastructure to implement technical controls — and whether those controls are policy-blocked or just unconfigured — determines which peer solutions are directly transferable vs. which would require new investment.

---

## Section IV — Standards Selection Decision Authority
**Goal:** Confirm who can approve the standards recommendation before Week 5 review begins. This is operationally critical — Deliverable 1 requires a named signatory and we cannot let Week 5 stall because the wrong person is in the room.
**Ask:** CoW project lead / City Manager's office

### Q1 — Single Most Important Output
What is the single most important thing CoW leadership needs to see from this benchmarking research to feel confident approving the Standards Selection Brief? Is it peer city validation ("other cities our size have done this"), professional association endorsement ("ICMA recommends this"), regulatory alignment ("this satisfies Kansas records law"), or something else?

> **Why this matters:** Knowing the decision criteria in advance lets us structure the evidence in Deliverable 1 to speak directly to what leadership needs. A brief that leads with peer city precedent lands differently than one that leads with regulatory analysis. We should write to the actual audience.

---

### Q2 — External Communication Requirement
Does CoW leadership need to communicate alignment to a single named framework to an external audience — such as a state agency, a federal grant program, an auditor, or the public? Or is the framework selection primarily an internal governance tool?

> **Why this matters:** If CoW needs to say "we follow DAMA" or "we are pursuing WWC certification" to an external stakeholder, that constrains the recommendation. A hybrid framework may be the right internal approach but require a single named label for external reporting. We need to know this before finalizing the recommendation language in Deliverable 1.

---

### Q3 — Sign-Off Authority
Who in CoW has the authority to formally accept or override the standards recommendation in the Deliverable 1 sign-off block? Please confirm their name, title, and availability during the Week 5 review window (May 5–9).

> **Why this matters:** All three deliverables are shared in Week 5 for simultaneous review. Deliverables 2 and 3 are already drafted against our internal recommendation by that point. If the sign-off authority is unavailable during Week 5, or if the wrong person is designated and cannot bind CoW to a decision, the May 16 delivery date is at risk. We need this confirmed before Week 2.

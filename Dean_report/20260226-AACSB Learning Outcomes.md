## What AACSB Requires

AACSB does not prescribe specific learning outcomes — it requires that the school:

1. **Define** learning goals appropriate to the program's mission
2. **Embed** assessments within the learning experience (not just surveys)
3. **Measure** student achievement against those goals
4. **Close the loop** — use results to improve the program, and document that improvement
5. **Maintain** a continuous evidence trail for the 5-year review cycle

IMPACT, as an applied learning initiative within a dual-accredited school, must demonstrate that participation produces **documented, measurable professional competencies** — not just experiential exposure.

> **2026-03-29 update:** The measurement system now uses three independent lenses per learning outcome to reduce self-evaluation bias while preserving AACSB pre/post evidence. Self-assessment (Lens C) is retained at 20% weight for AACSB continuity. Behavioral (Lens A, 40%) and Observer-rated (Lens B, 40%) lenses provide corroborating evidence. See `20260329-Objective-Measurement-Framework.md` for full specification.

## IMPACT Program Mission Alignment

**Barton School Mission:** Applied and experiential learning where students apply knowledge gained from faculty and business leaders while developing professional and life skills.

**IMPACT's contribution to that mission:** Students engage in real consulting engagements with Kansas businesses, applying cross-disciplinary business knowledge under PM supervision, and producing professional deliverables for actual clients.

**AOL scope:** IMPACT is an applied learning program, not a standalone course. The learning outcomes below are **program-level** outcomes, assessed through embedded instruments within the IMPACT workflow — not separate exams or coursework.

## The 5 Learning Outcomes

Each outcome is tied to AACSB's standard competency domains and assessed through a **3-lens composite score**. The composite replaces the single PM evaluation score as the primary achievement metric.

---

### LO1 — Professional Communication

**Statement:** Students demonstrate effective written and oral communication tailored to a professional business audience.

**AACSB Domain:** Written and Oral Communication

**Behavioral Indicators (what "achieving" this looks like):**
- Deliverables are clearly written, free of jargon, and appropriate for the client audience
- Weekly task reports are complete, specific, and submitted on time
- Student can present project progress to leadership or the client without excessive prompting

**Assessment Instruments — 3 Lenses:**

| Lens | Instrument | When | Who Assesses | Recorded In | Weight |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **A — Behavioral** | Weekly report non-flag rate (% not flagged by PM) | Every week (automated) | Power Automate | Weekly Task Reports — Reviewed by PM | 40% |
| **B — Observer** | PM BARS — Communication dimension (5 observable behaviors) | End of semester | Project Manager | Student Roster — PM BARS Score |  |
| **B — Observer** | Peer nomination Q1 ("explained ideas clearly") | End of semester | Teammates (anonymous) | Peer Nomination Form | 40% combined |
| **C — Self** | Self-confidence rating — Communication (1–5) | Pre and Post | Student | Student Roster — Pre/Post Score |  |
| **C — Self** | Knowledge test — Communication items (2 of 10 Qs) | Pre and Post | Auto-scored | Student Roster — Knowledge Test Score | 20% combined |

**Achievement threshold:** Composite Score ≥ 3.5 / 5.0

### LO2 — Analytical & Critical Thinking

**Statement:** Students apply analytical frameworks and evidence-based reasoning to diagnose client problems and develop actionable recommendations.

**AACSB Domain:** Analytical Thinking / Critical Thinking

**Behavioral Indicators:**
- Student identifies root causes rather than symptoms in client challenges
- Recommendations in deliverables are supported by data, research, or structured analysis
- Student can defend their reasoning when questioned by PM or client

**Assessment Instruments — 3 Lenses:**

| Lens | Instrument | When | Who Assesses | Recorded In | Weight |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **A — Behavioral** | Deliverable quality checklist score (5-item binary checklist per deliverable) | Per deliverable | Project Manager | Deliverable Quality Scorecard | 40% |
| **B — Observer** | PM BARS — Analytical dimension (4 observable behaviors) | End of semester | Project Manager | Student Roster — PM BARS Score |  |
| **B — Observer** | Peer nomination Q1 ("best analysis / explained complex concepts") | End of semester | Teammates (anonymous) | Peer Nomination Form | 40% combined |
| **C — Self** | Self-confidence rating — Analytical (1–5) | Pre and Post | Student | Student Roster — Pre/Post Score |  |
| **C — Self** | Knowledge test — Analytical items (2 of 10 Qs) | Pre and Post | Auto-scored | Student Roster — Knowledge Test Score | 20% combined |

**Achievement threshold:** Composite Score ≥ 3.5 / 5.0

### LO3 — Project Management & Professionalism

**Statement:** Students demonstrate the ability to manage their time, meet deadlines, collaborate within a team, and conduct themselves with professional standards.

**AACSB Domain:** Teamwork / Leadership / Ethical Conduct

**Behavioral Indicators:**
- Weekly task reports submitted on time ≥ 90% of weeks
- Deliverables completed by agreed deadlines
- Positive peer and PM feedback on reliability and team contribution
- Adherence to client confidentiality and IMPACT SOPs

**Assessment Instruments — 3 Lenses:**

| Lens | Instrument | When | Who Assesses | Recorded In | Weight |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **A — Behavioral** | Blended score: submission rate + deliverable on-time rate (auto) | Every week + per deliverable (automated) | Power Automate | Task Reports — Timestamp + Master Project List | 40% |
| **B — Observer** | PM BARS — Professionalism dimension (4 observable behaviors) | End of semester | Project Manager | Student Roster — PM BARS Score |  |
| **B — Observer** | Peer nomination Q2 ("most reliable and professional") | End of semester | Teammates (anonymous) | Peer Nomination Form | 40% combined |
| **C — Self** | Self-confidence rating — Professionalism (1–5) | Pre and Post | Student | Student Roster — Pre/Post Score |  |
| **C — Self** | Knowledge test — Project management items (2 of 10 Qs) | Pre and Post | Auto-scored | Student Roster — Knowledge Test Score | 20% combined |

**Achievement threshold:** Composite Score ≥ 3.5 / 5.0 AND submission rate ≥ 85%

**Behavioral Indicators:**
- Deliverables reference appropriate business frameworks or disciplinary knowledge

**Assessment Instruments — 3 Lenses:**

### LO5 — Ethical & Stakeholder Awareness

**Statement:** Students recognize ethical dimensions in business decisions and demonstrate awareness of obligations to multiple stakeholders — clients, community, peers, and the institution.

**AACSB Domain:** Ethical Understanding and Reasoning

**Behavioral Indicators:**
- Student respects client confidentiality throughout the engagement
- Student raises concerns about ethically ambiguous situations rather than ignoring them
- Deliverables consider impact on stakeholders beyond the immediate client contact
- No violations of IMPACT SOPs related to data handling, communication, or conduct

**Assessment Instruments — 3 Lenses:**

| Lens | Instrument | When | Who Assesses | Recorded In | Weight |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **A — Behavioral** | SOP training completion + zero violations (step-function score) | Week 1–2 + ongoing | Operations + PM flag | Onboarding Tracker + Violation log | 40% |
| **B — Observer** | PM BARS — Ethics dimension (3 observable behaviors) | End of semester | Project Manager | Student Roster — PM BARS Score |  |
| **B — Observer** | Client Y/N checklist item 5 ("respected confidentiality and boundaries") | Post-project | Client | Client Feedback Form | 40% combined |
| **C — Self** | Self-confidence rating — Ethics/Stakeholder (1–5) | Pre and Post | Student | Student Roster — Pre/Post Score |  |
| **C — Self** | Knowledge test — Ethical reasoning items (2 of 10 Qs) | Pre and Post | Auto-scored | Student Roster — Knowledge Test Score | 20% combined |

**Achievement threshold:** Composite Score ≥ 3.5 / 5.0 AND SOP Training Completed = Yes

## Assessment Instruments (Full Specification)

### Instrument 1: Student Self-Assessment — Lens C (Pre & Post)

**Delivered via:** Microsoft Forms
**Administered:** Day 1 (Pre) and final week (Post)
**Scored:** 1–5 Likert scale per competency

| Question | Competency | Scale |
|---|---|---|
| How confident are you in your ability to communicate professionally in writing to a business audience? | LO1 | 1 → 5 |
| How confident are you in your ability to analyze a business problem and develop evidence-based recommendations? | LO2 | 1 → 5 |
| How confident are you in your ability to manage your time and meet professional deadlines? | LO3 | 1 → 5 |
| How well can you apply concepts from your business courses to real-world problems? | LO4 | 1 → 5 |
| How aware are you of your ethical obligations to clients, peers, and your institution? | LO5 | 1 → 5 |

**Self-Assessment Score** = average of 5 ratings → stored in `Student Roster — Pre/Post Assessment Score`
**Self-Assessment Delta** = Post − Pre → positive delta is evidence of perceived learning
**Weight in composite:** 20% (combined with knowledge test — see Instrument 6)

---

### Instrument 2: PM BARS Rubric — Lens B (replaces impressionistic rubric)

**Delivered via:** Microsoft Forms (PM-only link)
**Administered:** Final week of semester
**Scored:** Observable behavior checklists — check only what you directly observed

> **Key change from prior rubric:** PMs no longer rate an *impression* (1–5). They check *observed behaviors* (Y/N). Score = checked items / total items × 5. This removes PM-to-PM scoring variance caused by subjective interpretation of scale anchors.

See `20260329-Objective-Measurement-Framework.md` for full BARS behavior lists per LO.

**PM BARS Score** = checklist score per LO → stored in `Student Roster — PM BARS Score`
**Weight in composite:** 60% of Lens B (combined with peer nomination — 40%)

---

### Instrument 3: Deliverable Quality Scorecard — Lens A for LO2

**Delivered via:** Microsoft Forms (PM-only link)
**Administered:** Upon PM review of each deliverable
**Scored:** 5-item binary checklist (Y/N)

| Item | Standard |
|---|---|
| 1 | Executive summary or overview section present |
| 2 | At least one recommendation supported by data, research, or named framework |
| 3 | Action items specify a responsible owner AND a date |
| 4 | Professional formatting: no spelling errors, consistent layout |
| 5 | Deliverable answers the client's stated question or scope |

**Score** = (Y count / 5) × 5, averaged across all deliverables the student contributed to
**Weight in composite:** Forms Lens A score for LO2

---

### Instrument 4: Peer Nomination Form — Lens B

**Delivered via:** Microsoft Forms (student-facing, anonymous)
**Administered:** Final week of semester
**Scored:** Nomination count normalized to 0–5

3 anonymous questions; student selects one teammate per question (cannot nominate themselves).
Nomination score = nominations received / max possible × 5.

**Weight in composite:** 40% of Lens B (combined with PM BARS — 60%)

---

### Instrument 5: Client Behavioral Y/N Checklist — Lens B + Lens A for LO4

**Delivered via:** Microsoft Forms (client-facing, post-project)
**Administered:** Within 2 weeks of project completion
**Scored:** (Y count / 5) × 5

5 observable yes/no behaviors; attributed to all team members.
Items 1–4 map to LO4; Item 5 maps to LO5.

---

### Instrument 6: Pre/Post Knowledge Test — Lens C

**Delivered via:** Microsoft Forms (student-facing)
**Administered:** Day 1 (Pre) and final week (Post)
**Scored:** Auto-scored, 10 multiple choice questions

10 questions across 5 domains (2 per LO). Score = (correct / 10) × 5.
**Lens C composite** = (Knowledge Test Score × 0.5) + (Self-Confidence Rating × 0.5)

## Scoring & Achievement Summary

| Learning Outcome | Achievement Threshold | Target Achievement Rate | Primary Lens |
|---|---|---|---|
| LO1 — Professional Communication | Composite ≥ 3.5 | ≥ 80% of students | Behavioral (report flag rate) + BARS |
| LO2 — Analytical Thinking | Composite ≥ 3.5 | ≥ 80% of students | Deliverable quality score + BARS |
| LO3 — Professionalism | Composite ≥ 3.5 AND sub rate ≥ 85% | ≥ 85% of students | Submission + on-time rate (auto) |
| LO4 — Applied Knowledge | Composite ≥ 3.5 | ≥ 80% of students | Client usefulness score + BARS |
| LO5 — Ethics & Awareness | Composite ≥ 3.5 AND Training = Yes | ≥ 90% of students | SOP completion + zero violations (auto) |

**Additional cohort-level metrics for Dean's report:**

| Metric | Definition | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Lens Alignment Rate | % students where all 3 lenses are within 1.0 of composite | ≥ 75% |
| Growth Confirmed Rate | % students where Lens A AND Lens B both improved post vs. pre | ≥ 70% |
| Self-Overrating Rate | % students flagged ⚠ (Lens C > Lens avg by >1.0) | Report; no target |

## Closed-Loop Process (AACSB Requirement)

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  1. COLLECT                                             │
│     Lens A (behavioral, auto) + Lens B (PM BARS,       │
│     peer nomination, client Y/N) + Lens C (self-        │
│     assessment + knowledge test) → SharePoint Lists     │
└────────────────────────┬────────────────────────────────┘
                         ▼
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  2. ANALYZE (end of each semester)                      │
│     Operations + Faculty Liaison review:                │
│     - Which LOs fell below target achievement rate?     │
│     - Where is self-overrating rate high? (coaching)    │
│     - Is Lens A+B growth confirmed across the cohort?   │
│     - Are Lens A and B aligned? (instrument validity)   │
└────────────────────────┬────────────────────────────────┘
                         ▼
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  3. DECIDE (leadership + faculty)                       │
│     - Update SOP or training for underperforming LO?    │
│     - Adjust BARS if PMs are checking/not-checking      │
│       inconsistently across teams?                      │
│     - Add mid-semester check-in if delta is small?      │
└────────────────────────┬────────────────────────────────┘
                         ▼
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  4. IMPLEMENT (next semester)                           │
│     Changes documented in SOP Library with change log   │
└────────────────────────┬────────────────────────────────┘
                         ▼
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  5. DOCUMENT (continuous)                               │
│     Semester AOL Summary in Hub SOP Library:            │
│     - Composite scores by LO (all 3 lenses shown)       │
│     - Achievement rates vs. targets                     │
│     - Lens alignment + divergence rates                 │
│     - Improvement actions taken                         │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

# IMPACT — Master Assessment Rubric
## Artifact-Based Evaluation | Spring 2026

**Purpose:** PM evaluates each student based on work artifacts already produced this semester — deliverables, weekly reports, emails, presentations. No new data collection required. Evidence already exists in your team SharePoint site.
**Completed by:** Project Manager (one rubric per student)
**When:** Final 2 weeks of semester — complete after reviewing the student's artifacts
**Time required:** ~15 minutes per student
**Submit to:** Operations by **[Deadline]**
**AACSB role:** This is the primary observer-rated evidence for LO1–LO5. Combined with the Focus Group Interview scores, it produces a complete assessment picture.

---

## How to Use This Rubric

**Step 1 — Gather artifacts before you score.** Pull up the student's weekly reports, any deliverables they contributed to, and recall key interactions. You are scoring evidence, not memory.

**Step 2 — Read each dimension and select the score that best fits** what you actually observed. Do not average up. If the student had one strong moment and three weak ones, that is a 2, not a 3.

**Step 3 — Write the artifact citation.** For each dimension you score, write the name and approximate date of the specific artifact or event that drove your score. This is the AACSB evidence trail.

**Step 4 — Complete the Summary Scorecard** at the bottom. This feeds directly into the Dean's Report data tables.

---

## Scoring Scale

| Score | Label | Standard |
|---|---|---|
| 4 | Exceeds | Student consistently demonstrated this competency at a high level across multiple artifacts or interactions. Client or peer would notice. |
| 3 | Meets | Student demonstrated this competency adequately. Evidence is present and credible. Minor gaps are acceptable. |
| 2 | Approaching | Student demonstrated this competency in isolated instances but inconsistently. Growth is visible but not yet reliable. |
| 1 | Does Not Yet Meet | Little or no credible evidence. Student did not demonstrate this competency at a professional standard this semester. |
| N/O | Not Observed | This dimension was not observable given the nature of the project. Do not leave blank — write N/O and explain. |

# The team's strategy for improving project data management focuses on several key areas:
*   **Establishing an "Impact Data Home":** The overarching goal is to create a robust, shared platform—likely utilizing their Microsoft Office 365 licenses to build a SharePoint site—that will act as a centralized "home base" for the operations and leadership teams to store information and develop standard operating procedures (SOPs).
*   **Centralizing Communications and Automating Workflows:** To move away from using personal emails, the team has established a unified email address (`barton.impact@wichita.edu`). They aim to automate workflows so that when client requests or student applications come through this email or the website, the information is automatically routed into SharePoint folders or used to schedule onboarding meetings. 
*   **Tracking Projects and Deliverables:** Currently, teams are using disparate systems like Trello to track their work. Leadership wants to streamline this by implementing weekly templated task reports to accurately capture and format project deliverables, as well as establishing a centralized system for capturing meeting minutes from all teams.
*   **Managing Student Data:** The system needs to track student employee metrics, including their majors, graduation dates, and completed training. It will also capture "success stories," documenting when students secure internships or jobs so the program can better demonstrate its impact.
*   **Digitizing the Onboarding Process:** The team envisions moving away from manual onboarding by digitizing the process. New hires could be directed to a digital platform or website linked to SharePoint to complete their self-assessments, HR paperwork, and name tag orders.
*   **Asset and Password Storage:** The central platform will also house media and marketing assets, such as student headshots and branding content for the website and social media. Additionally, the operations team plans to maintain a shared Excel file to securely store and manage passwords for their social media accounts.

# Constraints
* Microsoft Office 365 licenses

**Summary of the Workflow:**
*   **Step 1:** Create the SharePoint Communication Site "IMPACT Hub" (Tier 1). 
       * Why this product? Communication sites are designed for "one-to-many" broadcasting. They offer a clean, webpage-like interface with no "Teams" chat attached, making them ideal for a stable, program-wide landing page for SOPs, Branding, and Archives.
       * Key Feature: This is the site that IT will register as the "Hub" parent in the SharePoint Admin Center.
*   **Step 2:** IT registers it as a Hub, and create SharePoint Team Site (Stand-alone)
    * Why this product? Unlike a "Teams-connected" site, a stand-alone Team Site provides a secure, folder-centric environment for sensitive data (Rosters, Finance) without the "noise" of a Microsoft Teams chat. It is easier to restrict access to only 2–3 key individuals (Leadership/Ops) without creating an unnecessary Team in everyone's sidebar.
    * Supporting Product: Microsoft Lists (Used within this site to track the Master Roster and Project List).
*   **Step 3:** IT/Ops "Associate" your current separate Teams to that Hub.
*   **Result:** You have a unified network where students stay in their private project silos, but everything is visually and functionally connected to the central IMPACT organization.
